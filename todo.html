Culture

The Holi Festival is the festival of colors. It signifies the victory of good over evil and entering the spring season. It is a day full of spreading positivity, happiness, and love. It is meant to be 2 days long, but has grown into a week long festival. Many people go to Mathura to celebrate this festival, but there are other places in India you can go too, Rajasthan, Jaipur, and Pushkar. The colored powder, gulal has different meanings for being thrown on you such as red being love and fertility, blue is the colour of Krishna (Hindu God), and green is to represent new beginnings.

Amber Fort

If you have ever seen a bollywood movie, you will recognize this palace. If not, well you're about to find out that many bollywood movies are filmed in this palace. This palace is also known as Amer Fort, located in Jaipur. It started being constructed by Raja Man Singh I, was built by Raja Man Singh in 1589 and was renovated by Sawai Jai Singh in 1614. This is one of the main attractions in India where you get a beautiful skyline view of the whole Jaipur city. They even have some beautiful majestic looking elephants that you can ride to get that beautiful view.

FOOD

Who else loves spicy food?! There are so many locations in India where you can get street food in almost every state. Every state also has its own specialty in what they can make. When you think of Indian food, you probably think instantly of the spiciness like curry, but they are also known for their sweets and the sugar rush you can get and famous drink lassi. The awesome part about India is that you could be taking a walk and get hungry and it's there and it's cheap!
